\section{Summary}

The title of the song is the second to last line in the famous \href{https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%81%84%E3%82%8D%E3%81%AF%E9%A0%86}{Iroha poem} in Japanese literature

It is sung from \href{https://en.touhouwiki.net/wiki/Kaguya_Houraisan}{Houraisan Kaguya's} perspective --- an immortal princess living on the Moon. The song portrays her life of a princess in foreign land, with immortality being more of a curse than a blessing and the person who understands her the most is also the one that she resents the most --- \href{https://en.touhouwiki.net/wiki/Fujiwara_no_Mokou}{Fujiwara no Mokou}.

Princess Kaguya, along with the original game Imperishable Night, takes inspiration from `\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Tale_of_the_Bamboo_Cutter}{Kaguya-hime}' in Japanese folklore

The album has both Kaguya's and Mokou's themes separated into two tracks back to back, this is the first part of the two, prior to the song アンフルフィルド・プロミス 〜 果たされない約束. Further details of the two individuals will be stated in \hyperlink{backstory}{Translation notes section}

\section{Translation notes}
\hypertarget{backstory}{Kaguya and Mokou's backstory}: The method that Kaguya used to attain immortality was to drink the \href{https://en.touhouwiki.net/wiki/Hourai_Elixir}{Hourai Elixir} out of self-interest which was a taboo. Since she can't die from execution, she was then exiled to Earth and spend her immortal life down in Earth. When she was still on the Moon, she held a trial of `Five Impossible Requests' to seek for the suitable marriage partner, none of the contestants could complete such trial, including Mokou's father. Having her father and consequentially her whole family put to shame by the princess, Mokou bore hatred and later on also drank the Hourai Elixir seeking for revenge.

The full backstory of Kaguya and Mokou can be read in the Touhou Wiki \href{https://en.touhouwiki.net/wiki/Kaguya_Houraisan#Backstory}{here} and \href{https://en.touhouwiki.net/wiki/Fujiwara_no_Mokou#Backstory}{here}

\tlnoteref{1}: Very likely to be referencing the Hourai Elixir, as in the very next line, the `end' is considered thee end of her loyal lineage on the moon and the start of her immortality. To \href{https://en.touhouwiki.net/wiki/Hourai_Elixir}{quote} the Touhou Wiki: `According to Fujiwara no Mokou, the first taste of the medicine makes the drinker incapable of growing up or aging. The second taste makes them incapable of becoming ill. The third taste makes one completely immortal to the truest sense.'

\tlnoteref{2}\tlnoteref{3}: Direct mention of Mokou who can manipulate fire and often have her spells to resemble phoenixes, as they are the symbol of both fire and immortality.

Also quick note in the next line citing 「想ふ」 instead of 「想う」. This is old Japanese, which is actually quite normal considering that the speaker is Kaguya who is already over a thousand years old and also of loyal descendants.
